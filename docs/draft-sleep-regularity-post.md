## LinkedIn Version

8 hours of sleep doesn't help if it's 10pm-6am Monday and 1am-9am Saturday.

A UK Biobank study tracked 61,000 people using wrist accelerometers. They calculated a Sleep Regularity Index — how consistent your sleep-wake timing is day to day.

The finding: people in the most irregular sleep quintile had 20-48% higher all-cause mortality risk compared to the most regular sleepers. The effect held after adjusting for sleep duration.

Sleep regularity was a stronger predictor of mortality than how long people slept.

Most sleep advice focuses on duration. "Get 8 hours." But the data says consistency matters more. Going to bed at roughly the same time, waking up at roughly the same time — including weekends.

The irregular pattern is relatable because it's common. Late Friday, sleep in Saturday, early alarm Monday. Your body's circadian system doesn't adjust that fast. You're essentially giving yourself jet lag every weekend.

What to do about it:

Pick a wake time. Keep it within 30 minutes, 7 days a week. That single constraint does more than any supplement or sleep tracker feature.

Your wearable already tracks this. Most people have never looked at their sleep timing variance — only their duration.

It's free to measure. Free to fix. And it's one of the 10 health metrics with the strongest mortality evidence.
