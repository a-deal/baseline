## LinkedIn Version

My last post ranked the 10 highest-ROI health metrics by cost and evidence strength.

6 of the 10 are free. And one action closes all 6 at once: **connect your wearable.**

Sleep regularity. Daily steps. Resting heart rate. VO2 max estimate. HRV. Zone 2 cardio minutes.

Your Garmin or Apple Watch already collects all of this. Most people bought their watch for notifications and step counting. The health data sits in an app they open once a month.

The problem isn't collection. It's interpretation.

Your watch knows your resting heart rate is 68. It doesn't know that puts you in the 55th percentile for your age — better than average, but not where you want to be. It knows you got 7.5 hours of sleep. It doesn't flag that your bedtime varied by 3 hours across the week, which matters more than duration.

Each of these metrics has strong, independent associations with all-cause mortality. Steps: each additional 1,000/day correlates with ~15% lower mortality. Resting heart rate above 80 bpm: 51% higher mortality vs. the 51-60 range. Sleep irregularity: 20-48% higher mortality risk in the least regular quintile.

You already own the sensor. The data is already there. You just need something that interprets it in context.

That's what I'm building.
