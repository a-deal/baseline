What your current setup misses.

Every health tool covers a slice. Here's what each one leaves out — and the cheapest way to fill the gap.

Oura / Whoop user
You're nailing sleep and recovery. You've likely never had ApoB or fasting insulin tested. Those two add-ons (~$30) would fill your biggest blind spots. Your wearable also can't tell you about family history, blood pressure, or medication interactions — the things that change your entire risk model.
Quickest fix: Add ApoB + fasting insulin to your next lab order (~$30). Buy a home BP cuff ($40). Ask your parents about heart disease and diabetes. Total: ~$70, one afternoon.
Function Health member
160+ labs is impressive — you've got deep blood work covered. But you're still missing blood pressure monitoring, family history, and medication context — things no blood test covers. Function also doesn't track sleep, activity, or body composition, which make up ~25% of the foundation.
Quickest fix: Home BP cuff ($40). Family history conversation (free). Log your current medications (free). That closes the three biggest gaps Function can't touch.
Annual physical only
Your doctor ordered the standard panel. You probably don't know what was on it — most people don't. The standard panel typically includes a lipid panel, CBC, and metabolic panel, which is a solid start. But it almost never includes ApoB, fasting insulin, Lp(a), or hs-CRP — the markers with the strongest evidence for early detection.
Quickest fix: Call your doctor's office and ask for a copy of your last results — you paid for it, you own it. Next visit, ask for ApoB and Lp(a) by name. Most doctors will add them if asked.
Just your phone
Steps count. Literally. Each +1,000 steps/day = ~15% lower all-cause mortality. That's real data you're already collecting. But your phone can't measure blood pressure, blood work, or sleep quality — and you're almost certainly not tracking family history or medications.
Quickest fix: A $40 blood pressure cuff and one conversation with your parents gets you from ~15% coverage to 50%+. No wearable needed.
InsideTracker user
Strong blood + DNA combo. But like Function, it's missing the non-blood foundations: blood pressure, family history, sleep regularity, and medication context. The DNA component is interesting but the actionability of most genetic markers is still low compared to the foundation metrics.
Quickest fix: Same as Function — BP cuff, family history conversation, medication log. You've already invested in the expensive stuff. The gaps are cheap.

Baseline isn't a replacement for any of these tools. It's the layer that shows you the full picture — what you've covered across all domains, what's still missing, and what to do next. Different tools give you different slices. Baseline is the map.
